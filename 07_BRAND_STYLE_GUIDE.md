## II. VOICE & TONE GUIDELINES

### Overall Voice

- **Warm yet professional** (not clinical/cold)
- **Empathetic** (understands customer health concerns)
- **Helpful** (solution-focused, not pushy)
- **Transparent** (honest about prices, capabilities)
- **Local** (uses location references, community language)

### Tone Variations by Context

#### When Responding to Inquiries

- **Tone:** Warm, urgent, empathetic
- **Example:** "Hi [Name], thanks for reaching out! We have what you need and can deliver this evening. How can we help?"

#### When Providing Medical Information

- **Tone:** Professional, authoritative, clear
- **Example:** "Our pharmacist recommends taking this medication with food to minimize side effects..."

#### When Addressing Concerns

- **Tone:** Empathetic, solution-focused
- **Example:** "We hear you‚Äîslow response times are frustrating. That's why we've committed to replying within 1 hour. How can we help right now?"

#### When Promoting Services

- **Tone:** Enthusiastic, benefit-focused
- **Example:** "Same-day delivery now available for all orders over ‚Ç¶10,000! Get what you need by evening."

#### When Building Trust

- **Tone:** Confident, reassuring
- **Example:** "Licensed pharmacists available 24/7 to answer questions about your medications‚Äîfree consultation."

### Grammar & Style Rules

‚úÖ **DO:**

- Use conversational language ("we," "you," "our")
- Keep sentences short (15-20 words average)
- Use active voice ("We deliver medications" not "Medications are delivered")
- Use contractions ("we're," "don't") for friendliness
- Use specific numbers ("4.2-star rating" not "high rating")
- Use exclamation marks (sparingly) for enthusiasm
- Use bullet points for scannability
- Capitalize "Medplus Pharmacy" always

‚ùå **DON'T:**

- Use jargon without explanation
- Use overly formal language ("your esteemed patronage")
- Overpromise ("fastest delivery ever," "best pharmacy always")
- Use excessive exclamation marks
- Use ALL CAPS (except acronyms)
- Be pushy or salesy
- Ignore the customer's time zone or context
- Use outdated Nigerian English

### Sentence Examples

**Good Example:**
"Got an urgent prescription? Call us 24/7. Our pharmacists can usually fill it within 2 hours."

**Better Example:**
"Urgent prescription needed? No problem. Call 0905 549 2610 anytime‚Äîwe're always open and can usually fill it within 2 hours."

**Too Formal (Avoid):**
"In the event that your pharmaceutical requirements necessitate immediate attention, please do not hesitate to contact our pharmacy at your earliest convenience."

## IV. LANGUAGE & TERMINOLOGY

### Preferred Terminology

| Instead Of                | Use                                           | Why                  |
| ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| "Purchase"                | "Get," "Order," "Buy"                         | More conversational  |
| "Utilize"                 | "Use"                                         | More natural         |
| "Shall"                   | "Will," "Can"                                 | Modern English       |
| "Pharmacy retail"         | "Pharmacy services"                           | Clearer to customers |
| "Pharmaceutical products" | "Medicines," "Medications"                    | Easier to understand |
| "Avail yourself of"       | "Take advantage of," "Get"                    | More natural         |
| "Kindly"                  | "Please"                                      | More natural         |
| "In due course"           | "Soon," "Within 24 hours"                     | More specific        |
| "Dispensing"              | "Filling prescriptions," "Getting medication" | Customer-focused     |
| "Our esteemed customers"  | "Our customers," "You"                        | Warm, not flowery    |

### Regional Language Adjustments

**For Lagos/Nigerian Context:**

- Use "pharmacist" not "pharmacologist"
- Use "medicines" or "drugs" (common in Nigeria)
- Use ‚Ç¶ symbol for currency
- Use "WhatsApp" (very common in Nigeria)
- Reference local areas (Magodo, Ketu, Ikorodu Road)
- Use "today" rather than "this day"

**Avoid:**

- American English spellings (use British English: colour, centre)
- American cultural references
- Assumptions about healthcare access
- Formal "Sir/Madam" language (feels dated)

## XI. WHAT NOT TO SAY

### Medical/Health Claims to AVOID

‚ùå "Cures [disease]" (illegal claim)
‚ùå "Better than doctor's advice" (undermines medical authority)
‚ùå "100% guaranteed" (impossible promise)
‚ùå "No side effects" (false for most medications)
‚ùå "Works for everyone" (not true for all)

**Instead say:**
‚úÖ "Our pharmacists can explain the benefits and side effects"
‚úÖ "Always consult your doctor, and we're here to support"
‚úÖ "Recommended by healthcare professionals"
‚úÖ "Most customers report positive experiences"

### Tone Issues to AVOID

‚ùå Overly formal Nigerian English ("Your esteemed patronage")
‚ùå False urgency ("Hurry! Only 3 items left!")
‚ùå Dishonest claims ("Best in Lagos"‚Äîavoid superlatives)
‚ùå Clinical coldness ("Pharmaceutical dispensary")
‚ùå Excessive emojis or slang (especially in formal content)
